18. Revenue recognition
Revenue is recognized to the extent that it is probable that the economic benefits will flow to
the Company and the revenue can be reliably measured. Revenue is measured at the fair value
of the consideration received or receivable, excluding trade discount, rebate and sales return.
The following specific recognition criteria must also be met before revenue is recognized:
Sale of goods
Revenue is recognized when the significant risks and rewards of ownership of the merchandise
goods have passed to the buyer, usually upon the delivery of the goods.
Interest
Revenue is recognized as the interest accrues (taking into account the effective yield on the
asset)
