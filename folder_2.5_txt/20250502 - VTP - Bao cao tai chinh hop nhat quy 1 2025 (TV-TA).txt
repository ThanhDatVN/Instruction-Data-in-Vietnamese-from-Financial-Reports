2.3 . Basis for preparation of Consolidated Financial Statements
Consolidated Financial Statements are presented based on historical cost principle.
Consolidated Financial Statements are prepared on the basis of the summary of transactions arising at dependent account
member units and at the Company's office.
In the Consolidated Financial Statements of the Company, the intra-group balances and transactions related to assets, equity,
receivables and payables are eliminated in frill.
2.4 . Financial instruments
Initial recognition
Financial assets
Financial assets of the Company include cash, cash equivalents, trade receivables, other receivables and lending loans. At
initial recognition, financial assets are identified by purchasing price'issuing cost plus other expenses directly related to the
purchase and issuance of those assets.
Financial liabilities
Financial liabilities of the Company include borrowings, trade payables and other payables, accrued expenses. At initial
recognition, financial liabilities are determined by issuing price plus other expenses directly related to the issuance of those
liabilities.
Subsequent measurement after initial recognition
There are currently no regulations on revaluation of financial instruments after initial recognition.
2.5 . Translation of Financial Statements prepared in foreign currencies into Vietnam Dong
Financial Statements prepared in foreign currencies are translated to Financial Statements prepared in Vietnam Dong at the
exchange rates as follows: Assets and liabilities are translated at the closing rate at the end of the period: Owner’s equity is
translated at the exchange rate on the date of contribution. Items of Statement of Income and Statement of Cash flows are
translated at the actual exchange rate or average exchange rate in the accounting period (if the difference does not exceed 3%).

Pursuant to the provisions of the Securities Law and Circular No.
96/2020/TT- BTC dated November 16, 2020, of the Ministry of Finance on the
guidelines for information disclosure on the securities market, Viettel Post Joint
Stock Corporation (Stock code: VTP) provides an explanation for the after-tax ([^1^
profit in the first quarter of 2025, which has changed by more than 10% compared \U\ ^
to the same period in 2024 in both the separate and consolidated financial
statements, as follows:
1. Separate financial statements
Profit after corporate income tax in the separate financial statements for the
first quarter of 2025 reached VND 69.96 billion, an increase of 24.54% compared
to the same period in 2024 (the first quarter of 2024 reached VND 56.18 billion).
The main reason for the above fluctuations in the profit after corporate
income tax is mainly due to goods sold and services rendered. In the first quarter of
2025, revenue from goods sold and services rendered reached VND 4,769.14
billion, an increase of 11.44% over the same period in 2024 (the first quarter of
2024 reached VND 4,279.55 billion); faster than the growth rate of cost of sales. In
the first quarter of 2025, cost of sales reached VND 4,540.82 billion, an increase of
9.69% over the same period in 2024 (the first quarter of 2024 reached VND
4,139.87 billion).
Therefore, the profit after corporate income tax result in the separate financial
statements increased by 24.54% compared to the same period in 2024.
2. Consolidated financial statements
Profit after corporate income tax in the consolidated financial statements for
the first quarter of 2025 reached VND 69.34 billion, an increase of 18.62%
compared to the same period in 2024 (the first quarter of 2024 reached VND 5 8.46
billion).The main reason for the above fluctuations in the profit after corporate
income tax is mainly due to goods sold and services rendered. In the first quarter of
2025, revenue from goods sold and services rendered reached VND 5,041.6 7
billion, an increase of 7.86% over the same period in 2024 (the first quarter of
2024 reached VND 4,674.08 billion); faster than the growth rate of cost of sales. In
the first quarter of 2025, cost of sales reached VND 4,776.98 billion, an increase of
5.98% over the same period in 2024 (the first quarter of 2024 reached VND
4,507.25 billion).
Therefore, the profit after corporate income tax result in the separate financial
statements increased by 18.62% compared to the same period in 2024.
